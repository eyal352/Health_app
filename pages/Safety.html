Safety

For the majority of women with healthy, uncomplicated pregnancies, exercise is not only safe, it is healthy (see benefits). Always discuss it with your doctor before beginning any exercise program. Once your doctor has cleared you, you can begin exercising.

To keep both you and your baby safe, please keep in mind the following guidelines:

  • Always exercise in a relatively cool environment and avoid hot and humid conditions. If it’s hot and humid outside, find an air-conditioned, indoor space to exercise.
  • Before sure to drink plenty of cool liquids before, during and after you exercise.
  • Do not lie on your back or stomach.
  • If performing resistance exercises, only use a resistance that allows you to complete 12-15 repetitions.
  • Wear proper shoes to help avoid falls.
  • When stretching, be careful not to overstretch.
  • Never participate in contact sports or activities with a high likelihood of falling (such as horseback riding, mountain biking, skiing, and soccer).
  • Aerobic exercise is considered “moderate” if you can talk while exercising.

Do not exercise if any of the following applies to you, or you have any of the following conditions:

Have ever been told by your doctor not to exercise

Anemia

Heart rhythm irregularities

Bronchitis

Uncontrolled diabetes

Hypertension (high blood pressure)

Preeclampsia

Extreme morbid obesity

Lung disease

Have a multiple pregnancy (twins etc.)

Ruptured membranes

Placenta praevia

Have not discussed exercise during pregnancy with your doctor

Are extremely underweight (BMI < 18.5)

Have an intrauterine growth restriction

Uncontrolled seizure disorder

Are a heavy smoker

Uncontrolled thyroid disease

Orthopedic limitations

Heart disease

Incompetent cervix

Vaginal bleeding

Premature labor in current pregnancy

Stop exercising and seek medical care if you experience any of the following:

Vaginal bleeding

Shortness of breath before exercise

Chest pain

Muscle weakness

Calf pain or swelling

Dizziness

Headaches

Preterm labor

Decreased fetal movement

Amniotic fluid leakage
